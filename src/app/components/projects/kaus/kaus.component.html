Kaus: Designing a responsive ecommerce site

Overview:

Kaus has been in the insurance business for 30 years and provides all types of insurance for individuals. They currently work on a B2B environment through regional agents to sell their product.

Kaus wants to move from a B2B enviorment to a B2C environment. They want to pivot to the digital space, refresh their brand, and tap into the younger market.

Role:

UX Designer

The Process

Research

Competitor Analysis, Surveys, and Interviews were done to better understand industry standards, the clients, and most importantly the user.

I started the research process by conducted a competitor analysis. This was crucial since I am not a SME in the insurance space. This aided in understanding industry standards and expectations.

After educating myself in the insurance space, I then moved onto surveying and conducting interviews. The main goal was tounderstad and empathize with the user. To do this, I needed to know what pain points users encounter when shopping and purchasing insurance. What main factors contribute to their decision making? How can we as a company improve the overall user experience?

Needs/Wishes:

365/24/7 support.

Simple and straight-forward UI.

Noted privacy and secuirty were important.

Insurance dictionary.

Chat box feature.

Ability to bundle.

Frustrations:

Deciphering insurance jargon.

Too much fine print.

Too much text.

Struggled with ease of browsability.

Giving too much personal information.

Main Motivators:

Participants identified affordability, customer service, and ease of browsability as the three main factors they look for when considering an insurance company.

Through the reasearch, I was able to identify three personas. The first-time buyer, the middle class working parent, and the tech impaired. Since, the objective was to target the younger demographic, I focused on the first-time buyer and middle class working parent. Matthew Young was then created to further indentify with the users.

Information Architecture

A card sorting activity was done with users to find the best way to organize the content on the site. A site map was created based on findings of the card sorting.

Once the user had been established and the needs, wants, and wishes of the user had been identified-it was time to craft the information architecture of the site. I began by revisiting my research notes and the direct competitors sites. Since one of the main goals of the users was “ease of browsablility”, it was crucial that I let potential users be a part of this process. To include them, I conducted a card sorting activity.

I knew I would have two main navigations for ease of browsability. Additionally, some content like “Umbrella Insurance” would have to be under more than one category. Interaction Design

User and task flows were created to better understand the interaction between the user and the site. Responsive wireframes were then drafted based off the flows.

User flows and task flows were created to further develop what pages and forms would need to be created for the site.

With a better understanding of the interaction between our user and our product-it was time to draft responsive wireframes. With our persona in mind, I knew I wanted to keep the design straight-forward and easy to navigate.

User Interface Design

Responsive logos, a style tile, and UI kit were created to establish Kauses brand identity. Responsive designs were then created to bring the brand vision to life.

Brand refreshment was one of the main objectives of this project. Kaus realyed that they wanted their brand to have a modern, fresh, and trustworthy feel. Therefore, I set out to find colors, typography, and illustrations that embodied those keywords.

Now that the brand colors, typography and componenets were chosen and built out- it was time to bring the vision to life! It was time to create high fidelity responsive wireframes.

Iterate and Implement

A prototype was created and user testing was completed with four participants. An affinity map was made based off of their feedback. The affinity map aided in establishing priority revisions.

Key Takeaways

Research is vital in understanding what will differentiate you from the competiton. I learned from users one of the biggest headaches was deciphering through too much text and insurance lingo. Therefore, I focused on making a simple, straight-forward, task-based UI that met users needs. For example, I incorporated a help sidebar in the quote form process to guide and inform users along the way!

Get user feedback when choosing and deciding on brand colors, typography, illustrations, etc. I definitely found myself overwhelmed with so many options that I started expereincing creator’s block. But once I went back and started to ask for users’ thoughts and opinions, the brand really started to take shape.

Create and use a style library to your advantage! I made the mistake of wireframing my designs without creating a component library which left me to manually change every detail that needed iterating. But now I know how to create color styles, text styles, and component libraries and use them to speedline my process and my designs.
