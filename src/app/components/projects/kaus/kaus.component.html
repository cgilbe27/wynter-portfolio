Kaus:

Creating a feature that allows for student-to-student interaction

Overview:

Kaus has been in the insurance business for 30 years and provides all types of insurance for individuals. They currently work in a B2B environment through regional agents to sell their products.

Kaus wants to move from a B2B environment to a B2C environment. They want to pivot to the digital space, refresh their brand, and tap into the younger market.

The problem:

Kaus currently lacks an e-commerce site where they can sell in a B2C environment.

The solution:

Design a responsive, e-commerce insurance site that is approachable to a younger demongraphic. Refresh the overall brand.

Role:

UX Designer

The Process

Research

Competitor Analysis, Surveys, and Interviews were done to better understand industry standards, the clients, and most importantly empathize with the user.

I started the research process by conducting a competitor analysis. An analysis was crucial since I am not an SME in the insurance space. This aided in understanding industry standards and expectations.

After educating myself in the insurance space, I moved onto surveying and conducting interviews to further empathize with the users. The goal was to know what pain points users encounter, what factors contribute to their decision-making, and how can we as a company improve the overall user experience.

Needs/Wishes:

Ideally, they would like 365/24/7 support.

Want simple and straight-forward UI.

Noted privacy and secuirty were important.

Wished for some sort of insurance dictionary.

Wished for chat box feature.

Needed ability to bundle.

Frustrations:

Found frustration in deciphering insurance jargon.

Dislikes too much fine print.

Expressed displeasure in too much text.

Struggled with ease of browsability.

“Hates” to give too much personal information.

Main Motivators:

Participants identified affordability, customer service, and ease of browsability as the three main factors they look for when considering an insurance company.

Through research, I was able to identify three personas. The first-time buyer, the middle-class working parent, and the tech impaired. Since the objective was to target the younger demographic, I created Matthew Young to further identify with the targeted user.

Information Architecture

A card sorting activity was done with users to find the best way to organize the content on the site. A site map was created based on findings of the card sorting.

After identifying our users, it was time to craft the information architecture of the site. One of the main goals of the users was “ease of browsability". I found it necessary to conduct a card sorting activity. Once I quantified the results, I created a site map.

I knew I would have two navigations for ease of browsability. Additionally, some content like “Umbrella Insurance” would have to be under more than one category. This was due to a lack of consensus from users on how that should be categorized.

Interaction Design

For better clarity on how users would interact with the site, I had to construct and user and task flow. I primarily focused on the "get a quote" task and drafted wireframes based on the flows.

User flows and task flows were created to further develop what pages and forms would need to be created for the site. I focused on the “get a quote” task with this being an e-commerce site.

With a better understanding of the interaction between our user and our product-it was time to draft responsive wireframes. Keeping Matthew in mind, I knew I wanted to keep the design straightforward and easy to navigate.

User Interface Design

To refresh the brand identity, I designed logos, a style tile, and a UI kit. I then created hi-fi wireframes to bring the vision to life.

Iterate and Implement

To assess the flow of the site, I recruited four participants to participate in user-testing. I then created an affinity map based on the feedback.

A prototype was built out to conduct user testing. I focused on the “Get a Quote” task since that is the primary user action on insurance sites. Below is the Figma prototype used in testing.

Based on user testing, I created an affinity map to help prioritize final revisions. Overall, the design was well-received. Small touches based on feedback like adding more whitespace and having clarifying text further elevated the design.

Key Takeaways

Based on user testing, I created an affinity map to help prioritize final revisions. Overall, the design was well-received. Small touches based on feedback like adding more whitespace and having clarifying text further elevated the design.

I found myself overwhelmed with so many options that I started experiencing creator’s block. Obtaining user feedback when choosing and deciding on-brand colors, typography, illustrations helped streamline the design process.

Create and use style and component libraries as you go! Doing this will speed up my design process in future projects and minimize manual changes and frustrations.
